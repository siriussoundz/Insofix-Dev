Removal Process

Actual steps vary by project, but most enquiries follow a simple route from review to agreed scope.

1. Initial enquiry

Tell Insofix what has prompted the enquiry: sale, purchase, mortgage, survey comment, damp concern, access problem or planned insulation work.

2. Safe photographs and relevant documents

Send photographs only if they can be taken safely. Survey extracts, invoices, product labels and installation notes can help explain the background.

3. Initial remote review

The remote review considers the apparent material, extent, access and likely next step. It may identify that more photographs, a call or a visit is needed.

4. On-site assessment where necessary

A chargeable assessment may be recommended where depth, bond, construction, access or visible condition cannot be judged remotely.

5. Written quotation and agreed scope

Work is not booked until the removal area, preparation, limits, pricing and dates are set out and agreed in writing.

6. Access and work-area preparation

The work area is prepared to suit the agreed scope. This may involve access planning, protecting routes and confirming what needs to be moved.

7. Controlled removal

Material is removed from accessible agreed areas using methods suited to the surface and condition. The aim is to expose the relevant area without pretending hidden conditions are known in advance.

8. Cleaning and waste handling

Loose debris is cleared from the work area and waste is handled according to the agreed route and applicable requirements.

9. Identification of visible concerns

If staining, damaged materials, restricted ventilation or other visible concerns are found, they can be noted and discussed rather than hidden by replacement insulation.

10. Completion photographs and work summary

Where taken, photographs and a summary can record what was removed and which areas were included in the scope.

11. Replacement-insulation discussion

Replacement options are discussed after removal-area needs, ventilation and construction are understood.

12. Referral where appropriate

If the property needs structural, roofing, damp, electrical or surveying advice, Insofix may suggest an independent specialist rather than expanding beyond its removal role.

Ask about removal scope

If you are unsure which step applies, start with the property background and any safe photographs.
