How It Works

Our Spray Foam Removal Process

We use a careful, structured approach designed to remove spray foam insulation while protecting access to the roof structure and preparing the loft for inspection.

Step-by-Step Process

1. Initial Assessment

We review the loft space, foam coverage and general access to understand the scale of work required.

2. Preparation

The area is prepared for safe working, with attention given to protecting the surrounding loft space.

3. Manual Removal

Spray foam is carefully removed from rafters and timbers using methods designed to minimise unnecessary damage.

4. Clearance & Clean Up

Removed material is collected and the loft is left cleaner and more accessible for further inspection.

5. Survey Access Restored

Once complete, the roof structure is far more visible for surveyors, mortgage valuers or follow-on works.

6. Next-Step Advice

We can advise on the next stage after removal, including further inspection and insulation options.

Clearer Access. Better Visibility.

The main goal of removal is to allow the roof structure to be seen and assessed properly. This is especially important where mortgage lenders or surveyors require a clear inspection of rafters and timbers.

Every property is different, so the exact process can vary depending on access, foam type and coverage.

Need Advice Before Getting Started?
