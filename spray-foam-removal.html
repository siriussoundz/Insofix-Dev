Open-Cell Spray Foam Removal

Open-cell foam is usually lighter and less dense than closed-cell foam, but removal still needs planning around the roof build-up and accessible condition.

Typical characteristics

Open-cell foam is often softer and more flexible than closed-cell foam. It may still be bonded to rafters, felt, membranes and services, so the installation and construction matter.

Method depends on the roof

Removal methods vary with foam depth, adhesion, access, substrate condition and whether the roof includes older felt, modern membranes or non-standard detailing.

Protecting timbers and membranes

Controlled removal should prioritise avoiding unnecessary damage to roof timbers, felt and membranes. Aggressive shortcuts can create new problems.

Waste and dust control

Work should be planned with appropriate PPE, containment, dust control, bagging and waste handling for the material and site conditions.

Inspection after exposure

Removing foam can improve access to previously hidden areas. It does not guarantee that every concealed component is sound, especially where areas remain inaccessible.

Related guidance

If foam is dense, strongly bonded or on an older roof, read the closed-cell foam and older roofs guidance before assuming a simple removal route.
