Closed-Cell Foam and Older Roofs

Closed-cell foam is generally denser and more strongly bonded than open-cell foam. Older roofs may also contain aged or fragile components, so a survey-first approach is safer than promising complete removal automatically.

Why closed-cell foam can be difficult

Closed-cell foam may be significantly more difficult and time-consuming to separate from roof timbers, felt, membranes or other substrates. Aggressive removal can risk damaging the underlying structure.

Older roofs need context

Older roofs may already include aged felt, brittle materials, non-standard repairs, ventilation limitations or timbers that need careful assessment. Roof age alone does not determine the correct solution.

What must be considered

Roof construction, membrane condition, visible timber condition, ventilation, moisture indicators and how the foam was installed all influence the options.

Possible outcomes

In some situations careful removal may be possible. In others, localised repair, replacement of affected materials or a broader roofing solution may need discussion with an appropriate roofing or structural professional.
