Mortgage and Survey Issues

If insulation has been raised during a transaction, the useful next step is to understand exactly who needs what information.

Why spray foam may be raised

Spray foam in a roof space can prompt questions because it may cover timbers, affect access for inspection, alter ventilation routes or make the roof build-up harder to assess. A survey note does not mean every organisation will take the same view, and it does not automatically mean removal is required.

Different organisations ask for different things

Requirements may vary between lenders, valuers, brokers, surveyors, buyers and insurers. One party may want installation paperwork, another may ask for independent inspection, and another may want removal or further evidence. Where possible, ask the decision-maker for written requirements before commissioning work.

Documents you may already have

Helpful documents include the survey extract, valuation comments, installation invoice, product literature, guarantees, building-control correspondence, photographs from the installer and any recent roof or damp reports. These can help Insofix understand the enquiry without treating them as proof of suitability.

Separate roles in the process

The installer fitted the insulation, the surveyor comments on observed condition, the valuer advises a lender, and the lender makes its own lending decision. Insofix’s role is limited to reviewing potential removal work, explaining likely scope and quoting for agreed removal or replacement-insulation services.

What Insofix can assess and quote

Insofix can review photographs, inspect access and visible conditions where a visit is needed, discuss removal limits, and provide a written quotation for work within its scope. The quotation can describe preparation, removal areas, cleaning and records that may be supplied after completion.

What cannot be guaranteed

Insofix cannot guarantee mortgage approval, a valuation result, an insurance decision, a sale outcome or acceptance by a buyer. Removal may improve access for inspection, but third-party decisions remain independent and may depend on factors beyond insulation.

Questions to ask first

Before paying for work, ask the lender, broker, surveyor or buyer what outcome they need, whether partial information is enough, whether independent reporting is required, and whether replacement insulation must meet a particular standard. Sharing those answers helps avoid unnecessary or misdirected work.
