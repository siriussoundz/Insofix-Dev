Avoiding Spray Foam Scams

Most homeowners simply want clear advice. Be cautious of pressure-selling and claims that turn a complex roof-space issue into a guaranteed emergency.

Warning signs

  • Unsolicited calls or repeated pressure.
  • Claims that every spray-foam roof is immediately dangerous.
  • Guaranteed mortgage approval after removal.
  • Fake lender partnerships or false government approval.
  • Pressure to pay a deposit immediately or large payments before work starts.

Method concerns

  • Refusal to provide a written scope.
  • Removal recommendations without inspection.
  • No explanation of foam type or proposed method.
  • Automatically recommending full roof replacement.

Traceability concerns

Check company details, insurance information, written contact details and whether the contractor can explain who is doing the work and what evidence you will receive.

Questions to ask

  • Why are you recommending removal, remediation or further assessment?
  • What will happen if the foam is more strongly bonded than expected?
  • What evidence will be provided after the work?
  • Are you promising a lender outcome? If so, on what basis?

Balanced advice

This guidance does not mean all installers or removal companies are dishonest. It means homeowners should choose evidence-led advice over fear-based marketing.
