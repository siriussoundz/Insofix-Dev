Avoiding Spray Foam Scams

Calm consumer-protection guidance for checking contractor claims, written scopes and pressure-selling tactics.

Verify before you book

Ask what each claim specifically means and request written, verifiable evidence. A clear contractor should be able to explain method, scope, exclusions and practical risks without pressure.

Read how guidance, regulation and trade schemes differ.

Claims worth checking carefully

These claims are not automatically illegitimate, but they need careful verification, especially when combined with pressure-selling, vague wording or refusal to provide evidence.

  • “100% mortgage compliant.”
  • “Accepted by every major lender.”
  • “Removal guarantees mortgage approval.”
  • “Government approved.”
  • “Official advisory centre.”
  • “RICS approved.”
  • “We work directly for your lender.”
  • “A structural survey is included.”
  • “You must book today to protect the property.”
  • Large temporary discounts or countdown offers designed to force an immediate decision.
  • Claims that all spray foam roofs are dangerous, every property requires complete removal or full roof replacement is automatically necessary.

Important distinctions

RICS is not a contractor approval label.
Industry guidance is not the same as legislation.
A contractor’s completion information cannot control a lender, valuer or equity-release provider’s decision.
A “survey” should not be described as structural unless completed by an appropriately qualified professional acting within their competence.
A contractor should be able to explain the proposed method, scope, exclusions and potential risks.

Practical contractor checklist

  • Traceable company details.
  • Written scope and exclusions.
  • Insurance.
  • Relevant training or competence.
  • Proposed removal method.
  • Dust and waste controls.
  • Roof and timber risks.
  • What happens if unexpected conditions are discovered.
  • Payment stages and cancellation terms.
  • Evidence supplied after completion.
  • Whether third-party specialist input is genuinely independent.
