Frequently Asked Questions

Short answers to common homeowner questions about insulation-removal enquiries.

Does spray foam always need removal?

No. The answer depends on the material, roof construction, visible condition, reason for the enquiry and requirements of any relevant surveyor, lender, buyer or insurer. Some situations need paperwork or inspection rather than immediate removal.

Can Insofix guarantee mortgage approval?

No. Insofix can review potential removal work, quote for agreed scope and provide relevant completion information where work is carried out. Lending decisions are made by lenders and valuers, not by Insofix.

What is included in the free review?

The free review is a remote look at safe photographs and available documents. It helps understand the apparent material, extent, access and likely next step, such as more information, a call or an on-site assessment.

Are photographs mandatory?

No, but they are very useful. Without photographs, Insofix may only be able to give general guidance. If the loft or affected area cannot be photographed safely, explain the situation instead.

What is the on-site assessment?

It is a chargeable visit used to check access, material, extent, construction and visible condition so Insofix can define a removal scope and quotation. The fee is confirmed before booking.

Is it a structural survey?

No. The assessment supports Insofix’s removal quotation. It is not a structural survey, mortgage valuation, damp report or guarantee that a lender, buyer or surveyor will accept the property.

Can the assessment fee be credited?

Sometimes, but only if the written quotation confirms it. Any possible credit should be discussed before booking so there is no assumption about how the assessment fee will be treated.

Where does Insofix operate?

Insofix considers enquiries across England. Priority availability is initially focused across Southern England, and longer-distance visits may involve agreed travel or mobilisation costs.

What affects the removal scope?

Scope can be affected by material type, amount, bond strength, location, access, roof or wall construction, visible condition, contamination and preparation needed before removal can begin.

What records may be provided?

Records may include a work summary, before-and-after photographs where taken, details of the removal area and waste documentation where applicable. They describe the work rather than certify a lending outcome.

What happens if hidden damage is found?

Work may pause while the issue is documented and discussed. Depending on the concern, Insofix may recommend a roofer, surveyor, damp specialist or other appropriate professional before continuing.

What replacement-insulation options exist?

Options depend on construction, ventilation, access and the intended use of the space. Conventional loft insulation or a reflective system such as SuperFOIL may be considered where suitable and quoted separately.
