On-Site Removal Assessment

Some properties need an in-person visit before a reliable removal scope can be priced.

Why an assessment may be needed

Photographs cannot always show access, foam depth, bond strength, concealed areas, wall construction, contamination or safe working conditions. A visit helps define what Insofix can quote for.

What we look at

The assessment may consider material type, amount and location, access, roof or wall construction, visible condition, preparation needs and anything that may limit removal.

What is included

The output is information for Insofix’s removal scope and quotation. It can help identify likely removal areas, preparation needs, records that may be available and whether replacement insulation should be quoted separately.

What is outside the assessment

This assessment is used to define Insofix’s removal scope and quotation. It is not a structural survey, mortgage valuation or guarantee of lender approval.

What happens after the visit

Insofix may provide a written quotation, request further information, explain why work is not suitable, or suggest that an independent specialist should inspect a particular concern.

Assessment fee

The assessment is chargeable and the amount is confirmed before booking. Any credit against later work applies only if it is confirmed in writing in the quotation.

Travel outside the priority area

Priority availability is focused across Southern England. Longer-distance visits may include an agreed travel or mobilisation charge before the appointment is confirmed.

Booking, cancellation and rescheduling

Dates, access arrangements and any booking conditions should be agreed before the visit. Project-specific terms are provided before work or assessment is confirmed.

Book the right type of visit

Start with a remote review if you are not sure whether an on-site assessment is needed.
