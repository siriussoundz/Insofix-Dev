Regulation, Standards & Training

Spray-foam advice can be confusing because law, Building Regulations, retrofit frameworks, industry guidance and trade schemes are often discussed as if they are the same thing. They are not.

1. Statutory law

Legal duties can apply to consumer contracts, health and safety, waste handling and trading practices. A contractor should not describe voluntary guidance as legislation.

2. Building Regulations

Construction work may need to consider applicable Building Regulations, ventilation, thermal performance and safe detailing. Requirements depend on the work and property.

3. Retrofit frameworks

PAS 2030, PAS 2035 and related retrofit frameworks may be relevant in some funded or formal retrofit contexts, but not every private spray-foam removal job automatically falls under them.

4. Industry guidance

PCA material should be understood as industry guidance, inspection protocols, registers or schemes as applicable. The PCA is not a government regulator.

5. Trade schemes

Membership of a voluntary trade body or register should only be claimed where it is current and evidenced. This page does not claim Insofix is government, lender, RICS, PCA, TrustMark or PAS approved.

6. Training and competence

Homeowners should look for relevant training, insurance, safe systems of work, suitable PPE and dust control, understanding of foam types, roof-construction awareness and clear scope and exclusions.

7. Verified evidence

Good contractors provide honest before-and-after evidence and documentation for the work completed, while making clear that documentation cannot guarantee lender acceptance.

Questions to ask

  • What foam type do you think this is, and how have you assessed it?
  • What are the risks to timbers, felt or membranes?
  • What is excluded from your scope?
  • What evidence will I receive afterwards?
